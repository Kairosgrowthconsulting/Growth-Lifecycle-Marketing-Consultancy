Case Study 01 — Beauty & E-Commerce

How Lumé Beauty doubled lifecycle revenue and rebuilt their customer relationship in 8 months

Lumé was spending ₦4.2M/month on Instagram ads to drive sales, with a 12% repeat purchase rate and no email list strategy. Growth was expensive and fragile — one bad ad month meant a bad revenue month.

2.1×Revenue growth
68%Repeat purchase rate
₦1.8MMonthly email revenue
8 monthsTimeline

The Problem

Lumé had a genuinely excellent product — strong reviews, good social proof, and high first-purchase satisfaction scores. The problem was simple: customers bought once and disappeared. Without any lifecycle infrastructure, Lumé had no way to re-engage them.

Every new customer cost between ₦3,800–₦5,200 to acquire. Without repeat purchases, the business needed a constant stream of new customers just to maintain revenue — making it nearly impossible to grow profitably.

Their email list had 14,000 subscribers with an open rate of 8.2% — proof that the list had been neglected, not that customers weren't interested.

Our Strategy

We diagnosed the core issue as a lifecycle gap, not an acquisition problem. Before touching paid spend, we needed to build the retention infrastructure that would make every acquired customer worth more over time.

Our strategy was three-phased: first, rebuild the email foundation and activate the dormant list; second, architect the full post-purchase journey; third, introduce a loyalty mechanic that incentivised repeat behaviour.

Strategic priority: Move from transactional email blasts to a fully segmented, behaviour-triggered lifecycle programme — increasing both the frequency and relevance of every customer touchpoint.

Execution — What We Built

  1. List health audit & re-engagement: We segmented the 14,000-subscriber list into engaged, dormant, and unengaged cohorts and ran a 3-part re-engagement campaign. Recovered 3,200 active subscribers in 3 weeks. Open rate moved from 8.2% to 31.4%.
  2. Welcome series redesign: Built a 5-email welcome sequence that introduced the brand story, educated on hero products, and drove first purchase with a time-limited incentive. Welcome series alone now generates 22% of email revenue.
  3. Post-purchase lifecycle: Designed a 7-email post-purchase journey triggered by product category, delivering care tips, cross-sell recommendations, and a replenishment reminder at the right usage window for each product type.
  4. Abandoned cart & browse recovery: Implemented 3-step sequences for cart abandonment (37% recovery rate) and browse abandonment (18% recovery rate) — segments that previously had zero automated engagement.
  5. VIP loyalty segment: Built an RFM-based segmentation system that automatically identified top 20% customers and enrolled them in a VIP programme with early access, exclusive content, and loyalty rewards.
  6. Monthly campaign calendar: Developed a 52-week campaign framework aligned with product launches, cultural moments, and replenishment cycles — replacing ad-hoc blasts with strategic, planned communications.

Results — 8 Months

Email became Lumé's highest-margin revenue channel — contributing 38% of total revenue at near-zero variable cost. Repeat purchase rate climbed from 12% to 68% within 6 months of full lifecycle deployment.

Customer LTV increased 2.4× — meaning the same acquisition spend now generates significantly more revenue per customer, making paid ads profitable for the first time.

Monthly email-attributed revenue reached ₦1.8M, up from zero structured email revenue at project start.

What Made the Difference

The shift wasn't tactical — it was structural. By treating email as a relationship channel rather than a broadcast tool, every message became more relevant, more expected, and more welcomed by customers.

Lumé's founder noted that for the first time, she could predict revenue a month out — because the automated sequences created a baseline of consistent, reliable lifecycle revenue independent of ad performance.

"Kairos rebuilt how we think about our customers. Our lifecycle revenue doubled and we finally understood who our most valuable customers were." — Amara Adeleke, CEO, Lumé Beauty

Case Study 02 — B2B SaaS

How NovaTech NG cut monthly churn from 8.2% to 4.8% by fixing the onboarding experience

NovaTech had a product users liked — but they weren't reaching the "aha moment" fast enough. Activation rates were low, churn was high, and the business was spending aggressively on acquisition to replace subscribers it shouldn't have been losing.

41%Churn reduction
3.2×Trial-to-paid conversion
22%DAU increase
5 monthsTimeline

The Problem

NovaTech's churn rate of 8.2%/month meant they were replacing their entire subscriber base roughly every 12 months — an unsustainable and expensive cycle. Exit surveys showed the most common reason: "didn't understand how to use it."

Trial users were signing up, spending 2–3 days in the product, failing to complete a key workflow, and leaving — never seeing the core value the product was designed to deliver.

Our Strategy

We identified two intervention points: the onboarding sequence (Days 0–7) and the early lifecycle email programme (Days 7–30). Both were either absent or badly designed. No product changes were needed — this was a communication and journey architecture problem.

Core insight: Users weren't churning because the product was bad. They were churning because no one had shown them why it was good.

Execution — What We Built

  1. Activation milestone mapping: Worked with the product team to define the 3 key actions that predicted long-term retention. Users who completed all 3 within 7 days had a 94% 90-day retention rate. Users who didn't: 23%.
  2. Onboarding email sequence: Built a 9-email onboarding sequence triggered by user behaviour — not just time. Each email was designed to drive one specific activation action, with clear CTAs and in-product screenshots.
  3. In-app prompt strategy: Designed a series of contextual in-app prompts aligned with the email sequence — reinforcing the same milestones across both channels.
  4. Early churn detection: Built an automated alert triggered by inactivity at Day 3 and Day 7, prompting a direct personal outreach email from the NovaTech team to at-risk users.
  5. Trial-to-paid nurture: Redesigned the 14-day trial email sequence to lead with ROI evidence (case studies, time-saved metrics) rather than feature lists — shifting the conversation from "what it does" to "what it's worth."

Results — 5 Months

Monthly churn dropped from 8.2% to 4.8% — a 41% reduction. Trial-to-paid conversion improved from 11% to 35%, driven primarily by the redesigned nurture sequence and activation milestone programme.

Daily active users increased 22% despite subscriber count remaining flat — indicating that existing users were now extracting more value from the product.

Business Impact

With churn at 4.8%, NovaTech's payback period on acquisition dropped from 14 months to 8 months — making the business meaningfully more fundable and scalable. The same marketing budget now produces approximately 40% more annualised revenue per acquired customer.

"Before Kairos, we were spending heavily on acquisition with nothing to show for retention. The shift in how our unit economics look is night and day." — Kunle Okafor, COO, NovaTech NG

Case Study 03 — Home & Living E-Commerce

How Verdant Living turned a 1.4× ROAS into a 4.1× profitable acquisition engine in 6 months

A sustainable home goods brand spending £18k/month on Meta with a 1.4× ROAS — unprofitable even before factoring in COGS, fulfilment, and overheads. The issue wasn't the ads. It was what happened after the click.

4.1×Blended ROAS
£340kNew email revenue (annual)
52%Reduction in CAC
6 monthsTimeline

The Problem

Verdant's paid media was generating clicks at a reasonable CPM, but the landing pages were generic — the same homepage experience regardless of which ad or audience had been clicked from. Conversion rate was 0.9%.

More critically: there was no post-purchase strategy. Customers bought once, received a standard Shopify order confirmation, and were never contacted again. The business was paying to acquire customers and then immediately abandoning them.

Our Strategy

We identified three simultaneous opportunities: (1) improve landing page conversion by matching messaging to audience intent, (2) build a complete post-purchase lifecycle to drive repeat orders, (3) restructure paid campaigns around LTV-positive customer segments rather than volume.

Core hypothesis: If we could increase the average customer from 1.2 orders/year to 2.4 orders/year, we could halve our effective CAC without changing ad spend.

Execution — What We Built

  1. Landing page architecture: Built 6 dedicated landing pages matched to Verdant's top ad audiences — sustainability-focused buyers, interior design enthusiasts, gift buyers, and 3 product-specific pages. Average CVR increased from 0.9% to 2.6%.
  2. Paid campaign restructure: Rebuilt Meta campaigns around high-LTV lookalike audiences seeded from Verdant's top 15% customers (defined by 2+ orders). CAC dropped 52% while maintaining volume.
  3. Lifecycle build: Full lifecycle programme from welcome through win-back — 14 automated flows, 47 individual emails, personalised by product category purchased.
  4. Replenishment sequences: Product-specific replenishment emails triggered at the average usage window for consumable items (candles, cleaning products) — driving predictable, habitual reorder behaviour.
  5. Gifting campaign: Built a seasonal gifting strategy (4 campaigns/year) targeting the top 30% of customers for gift-purchase occasions — generating £94k in incremental revenue in Year 1.

Results — 6 Months

Blended ROAS across paid and email improved from 1.4× to 4.1×. Email contributed £340k in new annual revenue. CAC reduced by 52% as a result of better audience targeting and a dramatically higher LTV per customer.

Average orders per customer per year moved from 1.2 to 2.7 — exceeding the original hypothesis target of 2.4.

The Lesson

Verdant's issue was never the ads — it was the assumption that acquisition was the only lever. When retention improved, acquisition became more profitable automatically. The unit economics of the entire business changed without increasing a single pound of ad spend.

"The strategy session alone was worth more than six months of what we'd been doing on our own." — Fatima Nwosu, Founder, Verdant Living
